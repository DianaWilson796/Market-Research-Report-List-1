<p><p>The Car Active Head Restraints market can be categorized into two main types: headrest activation and seat back activation. Headrest activation involves a mechanism that moves the headrest forward during a collision to reduce whiplash risk by better supporting the occupant's head and neck. In contrast, seat back activation involves the entire seat back moving in response to impact forces, providing additional support and mitigating injury. Both systems aim to enhance safety and protect passengers during sudden stops or accidents.</p></p>
