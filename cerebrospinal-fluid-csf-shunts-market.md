<p><p>The cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) shunts market is categorized into two main types: fixed CSF shunts and adjustable CSF shunts. Fixed CSF shunts provide a consistent flow rate, effectively draining excess cerebrospinal fluid to prevent complications like hydrocephalus. Conversely, adjustable CSF shunts allow for modifications in drainage settings post-implantation, offering tailored treatment based on the patient’s needs and conditions. This adaptability enhances patient outcomes by accommodating changing cerebral pressure dynamics over time.</p></p>
