<p><p>Clean fire extinguishing systems encompass various market types, primarily including inert gases, perfluorohexanone, and other agents. Inert gases like argon and nitrogen work by displacing oxygen to suppress flames without harmful residues. Perfluorohexanone, a fluorinated ketone, effectively extinguishes fires by cooling the flames and interrupting combustion processes. Other agents may include water mist and foam systems that minimize environmental impact while ensuring fire safety. Each type offers unique advantages tailored to specific fire hazards and environments.</p></p>
