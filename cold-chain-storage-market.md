<p><p>Cold chain storage encompasses two main market types: chilled and frozen. The chilled market maintains temperatures typically between 0°C to 4°C, preserving perishable goods like dairy, fruits, and vegetables. This segment is crucial for extending shelf life while ensuring quality. In contrast, the frozen market operates at sub-zero temperatures, usually below -18°C, ideal for storing meat, seafood, and prepared meals. Both segments are essential for food safety, reducing spoilage, and enabling long-distance transportation of temperature-sensitive products.</p></p>
