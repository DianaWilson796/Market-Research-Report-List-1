<p><p>Dockless bike sharing can be categorized into two main market types: Point-to-Point and Distributed. In a Point-to-Point system, users pick up bikes at one location and drop them off at another, allowing for greater flexibility in travel routes. Conversely, a Distributed market employs a more evenly spread network of bikes, encouraging users to find and return bikes to designated areas, enhancing accessibility and reducing congestion in popular zones. Both models aim to promote sustainable urban mobility.</p></p>
