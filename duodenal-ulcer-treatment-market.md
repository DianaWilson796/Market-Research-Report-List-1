<p><p>The duodenal ulcer treatment market comprises various therapeutic options. Antibiotics target H. pylori infections, while proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) reduce gastric acid production, aiding healing. Antacids provide symptomatic relief by neutralizing stomach acid. Potassium-competitive acid blockers (P-CAB) offer an alternative mechanism for acid suppression. H2-antagonists also lower acid production but work differently than PPIs. Ulcer protective drugs help safeguard the gastrointestinal lining, and surgery is considered for severe cases that do not respond to medical treatment.</p></p>
