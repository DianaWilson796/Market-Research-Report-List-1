<p><p>Geothermal power generation encompasses various market types based on technology and resource utilization. **Back Pressure** plants use steam directly from geothermal wells to drive turbines and maintain pressure. **Binary** systems transfer heat to a secondary fluid with a lower boiling point, enabling power generation without direct steam use. **Single Flash** plants extract steam from high-pressure reservoirs, while **Double Flash** systems utilize two pressure levels for greater efficiency. **Dry Steam** plants harness steam directly from underground sources, and **Triple Flash** systems enhance energy extraction by employing three pressure levels for optimal production.</p></p>
