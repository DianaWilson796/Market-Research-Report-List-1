<p><p>The manual welders market comprises various types, including projection and spot welding. Projection welding uses the contact area from a raised feature on one workpiece to concentrate heat and join materials, making it efficient for parts with specific alignment requirements. Spot welding, on the other hand, involves localized fusing of overlapping metal sheets by applying intense heat at specific points, typically for automotive manufacturing. Both techniques serve distinct applications within industries, focusing on durability and strength in welded structures.</p></p>
