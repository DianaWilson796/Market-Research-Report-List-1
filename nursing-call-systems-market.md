<p><p>Nursing call systems are vital for enhancing communication in healthcare settings. Wired nursing call systems utilize physical cabling to connect patient stations to nurse stations, offering reliability and consistent performance. However, they can be more costly and time-consuming to install. In contrast, wireless nursing call systems use radiofrequency or Wi-Fi technology for communication, providing flexibility and easier installation. Both types serve to improve patient care by facilitating prompt responses from nursing staff, but they differ in infrastructure and operational dynamics.</p></p>
