<p><p>The phototherapy devices market is primarily divided into conventional and fiber optic phototherapy devices. Conventional phototherapy devices use traditional light sources, such as fluorescent and LED lamps, to treat conditions like neonatal jaundice and skin disorders. They offer high-intensity light but can be bulky. In contrast, fiber optic phototherapy devices utilize flexible fiber optics to deliver targeted light therapy, providing enhanced mobility and comfort for patients. These devices are increasingly favored for their efficiency and ease of use in clinical settings.</p></p>
