<p><p>The Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET) fiber market is primarily divided into two types: staple fiber and filament yarn. Staple fiber consists of short, cut lengths of fiber bundled together, commonly used in textiles for clothing and home furnishings. Filament yarn, on the other hand, comprises continuous strands of fiber, offering smoothness and strength, typically utilized in applications like industrial textiles and high-performance clothing. Both types benefit from PET's durability, lightweight nature, and resistance to moisture, catering to diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
