<p><p>The Positron Emission Tomography (PET) system market is categorized into three imaging types: direct, indirect, and alternative imaging. Direct imaging involves the direct detection of emitted positrons to create images, enhancing spatial resolution. Indirect imaging relies on a secondary detection method, often involving scintillation materials, to convert positron emissions into visible light for imaging. Alternative imaging includes advanced techniques like utilizing hybrid systems (e.g., PET/CT) or integrating other imaging modalities, enhancing diagnostic capabilities and offering more comprehensive patient assessments.</p></p>
