<p><p>The digital textiles market features various types of printing inks tailored for specific applications. Reactive dye inks bond chemically with fabric, ideal for cotton and other cellulosic materials, offering vibrant colors. Acidic inks, primarily used for wool and silk, produce rich hues due to their affinity for protein fibers. Paint inks provide versatility across different substrates, while dispersion and sublimation inks are used for polyester fabrics. Sublimation inks work through heat transfer, creating vivid, durable prints, particularly in sportswear and fashion.</p></p>
