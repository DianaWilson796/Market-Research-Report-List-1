<p><p>The raw pecan market is divided into two main types: in-shell pecans and shelled pecans. In-shell pecans are sold with their hard outer shell intact, appealing to consumers seeking freshness and longer shelf life. They are often used for cooking and baking or as snacks. Shelled pecans, on the other hand, are processed to remove the shell, making them convenient for immediate use in recipes or as toppings. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and culinary applications.</p></p>
