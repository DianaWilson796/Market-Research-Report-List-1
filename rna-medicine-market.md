<p><p>The RNA medicine market encompasses various types, including small interfering RNA (siRNA), antisense oligonucleotides (ASO), microRNA (miRNA), and nucleic acid aptamers. siRNA targets specific mRNA for degradation, silencing gene expression. ASOs modulate gene expression by binding to mRNA, preventing translation. miRNAs regulate gene expression post-transcriptionally, playing roles in various biological processes. Nucleic acid aptamers are short, single-stranded oligonucleotides that bind selectively to proteins or other molecules, offering therapeutic potential. Each type contributes uniquely to innovative treatment strategies.</p></p>
