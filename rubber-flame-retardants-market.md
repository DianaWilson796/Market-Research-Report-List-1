<p><p>The rubber flame retardants market is divided into three main types. Organic halogen flame retardants contain bromine or chlorine, providing effective fire resistance but raising environmental concerns. Organic phosphorus flame retardants are valued for their lower toxicity and efficiency in various rubber applications. Inorganic flame retardants, such as aluminum hydroxide or magnesium hydroxide, are non-toxic and environmentally friendly, often used for their thermal stability and smoke suppression properties. Each type serves specific applications based on safety, performance, and environmental impact.</p></p>
