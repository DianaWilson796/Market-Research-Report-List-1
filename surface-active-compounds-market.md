<p><p>The surface-active compounds market is primarily categorized into four types: anionic, non-ionic, cationic, and amphoteric surfactants. Anionic surfactants, negatively charged, are effective in cleaning products and detergents. Non-ionic surfactants, with no charge, are versatile and used in a range of formulations. Cationic surfactants, positively charged, excel in fabric softeners and antimicrobial applications. Amphoteric surfactants can carry both positive and negative charges, making them suitable for personal care items and gentle cleansing products. Each type serves distinct applications across various industries.</p></p>
