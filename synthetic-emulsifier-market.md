<p><ul><li>Ionic Emulsifier</li><li>Nonionic Emulsifier</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The synthetic emulsifier market is categorized into ionic and nonionic emulsifiers. Ionic emulsifiers, which can be cationic or anionic, contain charged groups that promote stability in emulsions, facilitating the mixing of oil and water. Nonionic emulsifiers lack electrical charge, allowing for greater compatibility with various formulations, making them versatile for both cosmetic and food applications. Each type plays a crucial role in enhancing texture, stability, and shelf life in industries such as cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and food manufacturing.</p></p>
