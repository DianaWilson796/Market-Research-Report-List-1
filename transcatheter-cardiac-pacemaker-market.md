Left Ventricular Pacemakers are designed to stimulate the left ventricle directly, improving cardiac synchrony and enhancing heart function in patients with heart failure. In contrast, Right Ventricular Pacemakers focus on modulating the heart's electrical activity primarily through the right ventricle. Both types play crucial roles in managing arrhythmias and optimizing heart performance, addressing specific patient needs and conditions.</p></p>
