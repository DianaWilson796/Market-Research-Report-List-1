<p><p>The veterinary vaccines market comprises several types. Inactivated vaccines use killed pathogens to provoke an immune response. Live attenuated vaccines employ weakened forms of the pathogen for immunity. Conjugate vaccines link antigens to enhance response effectiveness, while toxoid vaccines use inactivated toxins. Recombinant vaccines utilize genetically engineered elements to stimulate immunity, and DNA vaccines incorporate DNA sequences from pathogens to generate an immune response. Other vaccines may include novel approaches and formulations, expanding the market's diversity.</p></p>
